UMSI Tutoring & Academic Support

Welcome!

The Academic Success Team at UMSI is dedicated to supporting students inside and outside of the classroom. This site is dedicated to connecting students to tutoring and academic support resources

We work to break down barriers and increase access through funding. For U-M students, staff, faculty, and surrounding community, we offer opportunities for scholarships, emergency grants, sponsorships, fellowships, and more for a range of situations and life circumstances.

UMSI Peer Tutoring

In partnership with our UMSI faculty, the UMSI Peer Tutoring program is an opportunity for UMSI students and students taking UMSI courses to engage with peer tutors and receive assistance and support in UMSI courses.

Programming Peer Tutoring

UMSI Programming Peer Tutoring provides support for students enrolled in key programming courses and general Python-related topics. This resource is available for the following courses:

  • SI 106: Programs, Information, and People
  • SI 206: Data-Oriented Programming
  • SI 506: Programming I
  • SI 507: Intermediate Programming

Math Peer Tutoring

UMSI Math Peer Tutoring provides support for both SI and SIADS courses, as well as general math-related topics. The program offers assistance in the following subject areas:

  • Linear Algebra
  • Statistics
  • General Math related course support
  • SIADS 502: Math Methods I

Additional Academic Support Resources

  • Sweetland Writing Center: Offers personalized tutoring and workshops to improve writing skills for undergraduate and graduate students. Specialized support is available for multilingual and international students.
  • English Language Institute (ELI): Provides English for Academic Purposes courses, GSI preparation, and TESOL training to help students excel in academic and teaching environments.
  • LS&A Math Lab: Free tutoring services for mathematics courses numbered up to 217, with flexible support for questions from any U-M student.
  • Services for Students with Disabilities (SSD): Promotes an inclusive and equitable educational experience for students with disabilities through tailored resources and accommodations.
